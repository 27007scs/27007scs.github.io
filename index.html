The AI Regulation and Public Protection Act

The problem

AI is growing faster than the rules around it. Without clear regulation, companies and institutions can deploy powerful AI systems before the public understands the risks.

Main rules

High-risk AI systems must be registered, tested for safety and bias, clearly disclosed to the public, and subject to human oversight before they can be widely used.

Who benefits

Workers, students, families, consumers, and small businesses benefit when AI is introduced with clear limits instead of being allowed to spread without accountability.

Criticisms

Critics may argue that regulation slows innovation, creates new compliance costs, or makes it harder for companies to compete quickly in the global AI market.

Why voters should support it

Voters should support it because powerful technology should answer to the public. Regulation helps make sure AI serves people safely, fairly, and transparently instead of operating without meaningful rules.

The Biased Algorithm Accountability Act

The problem

AI systems used in hiring, lending, insurance, and housing can reproduce unfair discrimination at scale if they are not tested carefully.

Main rules

Companies must run independent bias audits, keep records of how models are evaluated, notify affected users, and pause use when serious disparities are found.

Who benefits

Job applicants, renters, borrowers, and historically excluded communities benefit when unfair automated systems are challenged before they become routine.

Criticisms

Opponents may argue that audits are expensive, standards are hard to define, or compliance costs could burden smaller companies.

Why voters should support it

A person should not lose a job, loan, or home because of a hidden and untested algorithm. Fairness rules help protect equal opportunity in everyday life.

The Deepfake and AI Deception Prevention Act

The problem

Deepfakes and synthetic media can spread misinformation, damage reputations, and confuse voters by making fake content look real.

Main rules

AI-generated political ads and high-risk synthetic media must be labeled, harmful impersonation is restricted, and platforms must respond quickly to verified deceptive content.

Who benefits

Voters, candidates, students, creators, and ordinary people who could otherwise be targeted by fake images, voice clones, or misleading videos.

Criticisms

Some critics may worry about free speech issues, over-moderation, or whether platforms can enforce the rules consistently.

Why voters should support it

Democracy works best when people can tell what is real. Basic labeling and anti-deception rules help protect elections, trust, and personal safety.
