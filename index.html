Proposed law guide

A simple proposal for regulating artificial intelligence.

The Safe and Fair AI Act

The problem

AI systems can be rushed into schools, workplaces, hospitals, banks, and government services before they are proven safe, fair, or accurate.

Main rules

High-risk AI systems must be tested before public use, clearly labeled when they affect people, reviewed by humans in major decisions, and paused if they are shown to cause serious harm.

Who benefits

Workers, students, families, patients, renters, borrowers, and everyday consumers all benefit when powerful AI tools have to meet basic public standards.

Criticisms

Critics may say the law slows innovation, adds costs for companies, and creates more government oversight than some businesses want.

Why voters should support it

Voters should support it because no company should be allowed to experiment on the public with powerful AI systems. Basic guardrails help protect fairness, safety, and accountability.

Why it matters

This proposal shows what a clear AI law could look like.

It is easy to understand

The proposal focuses on a simple idea: if AI can seriously affect people, it should meet stronger rules before it is used.

It puts people first

The law is built around safety, fairness, and human review rather than letting speed and profit decide everything.

It creates accountability

Companies that build or use risky AI should have to explain it, test it, and answer for the harm it causes.

FAQ

Basic questions voters may ask about AI laws.

Is this a real law?

No. This is a proposed law created for this website to show what a strong but simple AI regulation plan could look like.

Why make up a law instead of using a real one?

A proposed law lets the site focus on the exact rules and values you want to support without being tied to an existing government bill.

Can we add more real laws later?

Yes. We can later compare this proposal to real laws from U.S. states, Congress, or other countries.
